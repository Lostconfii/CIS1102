Overview of Motherboards

Definition: The motherboard is the central component connecting all parts of a computer, serving as the platform for the CPU, memory, storage devices, and expansion cards.

Form Factors

Importance: The form factor determines the size, shape, and layout of the motherboard, which affects compatibility with cases.

Common Types:

  • ATX (Advanced Technology Extended): The largest standard size, offering flexibility and expansion options.
  • Micro-ATX: A smaller version suitable for compact systems.
  • Mini-ITX: The smallest form factor ideal for ultra-compact builds.

CPU and Memory Support

CPU Sockets: Motherboards support specific CPU sockets for various families like Intel LGA 1700 or AMD AM5.

Memory Slots: Typically support DDR4 or DDR5 RAM, varying in number and type.

Chipsets and BIOS

Chipset Functionality: Manages communication between the CPU, memory, and peripherals.

BIOS: Firmware that initiates the boot process and controls hardware settings.

Expansion Capabilities

PCI Slots: Allow for the addition of graphics cards, sound cards, and other peripherals.

SATA Connectivity: Connects storage devices like hard drives and SSDs.
